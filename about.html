About the mAIques Lab

Our mission is to decode the structural and spatial complexity of the extracellular matrix (ECM) to enable earlier cancer detection and personalized therapies through spatial omics and AI.

Research Themes

  • ECM plasticity and mechanobiology during early tumor development
  • Spatial tissue organization and single-cell technologies
  • 3D aligned ECM models and organotypic cultures
  • AI-driven histopathology and biomarker discovery
  • Translational precision oncology using large-scale clinical datasets

Lab Origins & Vision

I studied Biotechnology with a specialization in health sciences and completed my PhD in a clinical pathology lab at the University of Lleida, under the mentorship of Prof. Xavier Matias-Guiu and Prof. Rosa Maria Marti. I pursued postdoctoral training in 3D and spatial biology across King’s College London, Barts Cancer Institute, and the Institute of Cancer Research with Prof. Victoria Sanz-Moreno.

The mAIques Lab was founded in 2024 through the CRUK Career Establishment Award (PRECISE programme), under the mentorship of Prof. Claude Chelala and Prof. Carol Dezateux. It was established as part of the Barts Precision Medicine Programme within the Barts Life Sciences ecosystem and generously supported by Barts Charity.

Our team combines spatial biology, bioinformatics, and translational research to accelerate the identification of ECM-driven biomarkers that guide early detection, stratification, and treatment prediction in melanoma, pancreatic, and breast cancer.

Collaborations & Impact

We are part of the OPTIMA Consortium (European Integrative Medicine Initiative) and collaborate with partners including Panakeia Technologies, exploring AI-powered solutions in pathology. These partnerships help us bridge lab-based discovery and real-world clinical implementation, particularly within the diverse populations of East London.

Where to Find Us

Our lab is based at the Centre for Cancer Biomarkers & Biotherapeutics, Barts Cancer Institute, part of Queen Mary University of London.
